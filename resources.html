About Domain Spoofing

Domain spoofing is when attackers create fake domains that resemble legitimate ones to trick users. Common techniques include:

  • Homograph attacks: Using similar-looking characters (e.g., "goog1e.com")
  • Subdomain spoofing: Creating "yourcompany.phishingsite.com"
  • Typosquatting: Registering common misspellings

Our scanner checks for these threats by analyzing DNS records, SPF/DKIM/DMARC configurations, and known phishing databases.

Cybersecurity Definitions

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber attack technique where attackers impersonate legitimate entities to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as login credentials, credit card numbers, or other personal data. These attacks typically occur through email, but can also happen via text messages (smishing) or phone calls (vishing).

What is Vishing?

Vishing (voice phishing) is a type of phishing attack conducted over phone calls. Attackers pretend to be from legitimate organizations like banks, government agencies, or tech support to trick victims into providing sensitive information or granting access to systems.

What is Smishing?

Smishing (SMS phishing) is a phishing variant that uses text messages to trick recipients into clicking malicious links, downloading malware, or providing sensitive information. These messages often appear to come from trusted sources and create a sense of urgency.

What is Malvertising?

Malvertising (malicious advertising) is a cyber attack technique that leverages online advertising networks to distribute malware. Unlike traditional malware attacks that require user action, malvertising injects malicious code into legitimate ad networks, potentially infecting users who simply visit compromised websites.

How Do Cybercriminals Exploit Malvertising?

Attackers use several techniques:

  • Infiltrating authentic advertisements with malicious code
  • Creating entirely new malicious ads designed to evade detection
  • Using sophisticated tactics to target specific user groups
  • Exploiting vulnerabilities in the ad supply chain

Common Malvertising Attack Techniques:

  1. Exploit Kits: Target vulnerabilities in browsers/plugins
  2. Phishing Ads: Redirect to fake sites to steal credentials
  3. Drive-by Downloads: Automatically download malware
  4. Forced Redirects: Send users to malicious sites
  5. Cryptojacking Scripts: Mine cryptocurrency without consent
  6. Scareware: Fake warnings to install malware
What is a Phishing Simulation?

A phishing simulation is a controlled exercise where organizations send mock phishing emails to employees to test their ability to recognize and respond to phishing attempts. These simulations help identify vulnerabilities in employee awareness and measure the effectiveness of security training programs.

What is Executive Phishing?

Executive phishing (also called CEO fraud or whaling) targets high-level executives with sophisticated phishing attacks. These often involve impersonating the CEO or other executives to trick employees into transferring money or sharing sensitive company information.

Guides & Best Practices

Phishing Awareness Training

Investing in phishing awareness training is essential to safeguarding your workforce and fulfilling regulatory requirements. Take proactive steps to educate your employees through accessible, dynamic, and interactive training sessions.

What Is Phishing Awareness Training?

Phishing awareness training is an educational program designed to teach individuals how to recognize and respond to phishing attempts effectively. It typically involves simulated phishing attacks, interactive modules, and educational resources to help employees understand the tactics used by cybercriminals and how to avoid falling victim to them.

What Are The Elements of Phishing Awareness Training?

The elements of effective phishing awareness training include:

  1. Interactive Modules: Covering identification of phishing emails and malicious links
  2. Simulated Phishing Campaigns: Realistic tests of employee awareness
  3. Real-life Examples: Case studies of actual phishing attempts
  4. Best Practices: Guidelines for handling suspicious communications
  5. Reporting Procedures: Clear instructions for reporting threats
  6. Ongoing Education: Regular updates about new phishing tactics

What Are Open-Source Phishing Tools?

Developing a Comprehensive Training Program

Create an impactful phishing awareness training program at no cost with these strategies:

Simulate Real-World Scenarios

An essential component involves replicating genuine phishing attacks. Through these simulated scenarios, employees experience controlled environments that mirror real-life cyber threats. This exposure equips them with the knowledge and skills to recognize and address actual phishing attempts, ultimately bolstering their cybersecurity readiness.

Customize Your Training

Adapt phishing programs to suit employees across various industries. For instance, individuals in financial services may encounter distinct risks compared to those in government roles. By customizing training, employees can gain insights into identifying and addressing risks specific to their industry.

Conduct Periodic Training

Consistency is paramount for successful phishing awareness training. Regular updates and ongoing reinforcement are essential to sustain employees' alertness and knowledge, enabling them to identify and thwart evolving phishing tactics effectively.

Several compliance frameworks mandate routine training including:

  • NIST 800-171
  • NIST 800-53
  • Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification
  • ISO27001
